---
title: User quickstart (5 min)
description: Connect with Privy, recharge $1 of USDsui, call a tool, and inspect the on-chain receipt.
---

This path takes you from zero to a settled, on-chain MCP tool call in about
five minutes. You need a browser and roughly $1.

## 1. Connect (≈1 min)

Open [mcpx.gg](https://mcpx.gg) and click **Connect**. Authentication is
via **Privy** — choose any of:

- An external Sui wallet (Suiet, Slush, Surf, Phantom-Sui).
- Social login: Google, email, Apple, Discord, or Twitter. Privy
  provisions an embedded Sui wallet for you; you never handle a seed
  phrase.

A first-time wallet is auto-granted a small **subsidy** (a bootstrap
USDsui grant from the platform, default 1.0 USDsui) so you can try a call
before funding anything.

## 2. Recharge $1 (≈1 min)

Go to **Dashboard → Recharge**. Add **1 USDsui**. Under the hood this
calls `session::deposit` on your **Session** — a pre-funded escrow object
the settlement contract debits per call. Balances are tracked in **atomic
units**: USDsui has 6 decimals, so 1 USDsui = `1_000_000` atomic.

> You are never charged a credit card. There is no subscription. You spend
> exactly the per-call price of the tools you call, plus the 2.5% take
> rate, and nothing else.

## 3. Call a tool (≈2 min)

Open the [Marketplace](https://mcpx.gg/marketplace) and pick a server —
e.g. **`walrus-search`**. On the server detail page, the embedded
`<mcpx-call>` widget lets you run a tool right there. Try `query` with
`{"q":"sui"}` and click **Call**.

When you click Call:

1. The gateway authorizes your session key.
2. The **facilitator** builds one Sui programmable transaction block that
   debits your session, splits the fee (dev / treasury / insurance), and
   mints your receipt.
3. The tool result and the receipt come back inline.

## 4. See the receipt (≈1 min)

The widget shows a **receipt** block with a `txDigest`. Open it in a Sui
explorer, or visit **Dashboard → Receipts**. You will see a soulbound
`CallReceipt` object recording the payer, server, amount, the three fee
shares, and a Walrus `blobId` pointing at the permanently-retained call
log.

That receipt can never be transferred or deleted. It is your permanent,
verifiable proof the call happened and was paid for.
